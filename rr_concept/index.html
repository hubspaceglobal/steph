Overview
Concept Summary
This document presents a concept for organizing a cross-functional team into a structured association capable of delivering a coordinated, rapid-response supply chain. The association operates through a tiered governance model in which a Steering Committee provides strategic oversight and working groups execute operational scope across defined supply chain functions. Membership is restricted to operators active on the African continent and is subject to a formal vetting and onboarding process.

The association is designed to function as both a coordinating body and a data aggregation entity, enabling member organizations to contribute to a bioscience global intelligence system while maintaining enforceable contractual obligations and equitable access to funding.
Working Groups
Active Working Groups

Two working groups execute the operational scope of the association. Each group is composed of specialized roles aligned to a defined area of the supply chain mission.

Working Group 01
BioSciTech

A cross-functional innovation team designing new approaches to rapid bioscience development, disaster preparedness, and precision health response across the African continent. The group comprises Research Scientists, BioEngineers, BioStat Intelligence Specialists, and Lab Technicians working in concert to accelerate discovery and field deployment.

Intelligence
Global Intelligence System

A data aggregation platform fed by member operational reporting. Enables the association to monitor supply chain performance, identify gaps, and support evidence-based rapid-response decisions.

The association is designed to function as both a coordinating body and a data aggregation entity. Supply chain members report operational data to the bioscience global intelligence system, and the association aggregates and publishes intelligence outputs. This enables real-time monitoring of supply chain performance, outbreak signals, and intervention effectiveness across the network.

The Global Intelligence System is architected by the BioStat Intelligence Specialist within the BioSciTech Working Group, and is sustained through enforceable data-reporting obligations embedded in member contracts.

Structure
Organizational Structure

The association is organized across four structural domains: Governance, Treasury, Membership, and Projects. Each domain carries defined rules and conditions that govern participation and operations within the network.

01 — Governance
Voting and Quorum

The association operates under a formal governance framework designed to ensure accountability and broad member representation in decision-making. Voting thresholds are set to require meaningful participation and strong consensus before any resolution passes.

02 — Treasury
Management Budget

Association management costs are funded through a percentage of total supply chain funds. The management budget is capped to ensure the majority of resources are directed to operational supply chain members rather than administrative overhead.

Management fees cover: legal, accounting, technical support, communications, and project management.

03 — Membership
Eligibility and Onboarding

Membership is restricted to organizations that meet specific operational and geographic criteria. Admission is not automatic and requires completion of a structured vetting and approval process.

  • All supply chain members must operate within Africa
  • Prospective members are onboarded through a working group vetting process
  • Final admission requires Steering Committee approval
  • No organization may participate prior to completing this process
04 — Projects
Submission and Review

Project submission is a member-exclusive function. This restriction ensures that projects originate from within the supply chain network and are grounded in operational context and validated expertise.

  • Only current members of the association may submit projects
  • Project submissions are reviewed through established governance process
